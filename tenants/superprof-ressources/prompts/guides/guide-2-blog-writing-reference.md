# SEO Elements for Content Optimisation

These elements tell search engines and users what your article is about. Each has strict character limits—exceeding them means your text gets cut off in search results. Here are the guidelines on how we use them for the blog:

**Title**

This is what appears in Google search results. Its job is to attract the click.

- Must contain the main keyword (from your blog order)
- Maximum 60 characters (including spaces)
- Must accurately represent the content

**Generating a title with AI (upcoming feature)**

1. **Fill in the Main Keyword field** in the SEO AI Generator panel.
2. **Click the ✨ button** next to the field.
3. **Review the 5 title options** shown inside the modal (each option already includes your main keyword).
4. Select your preferred option by clicking it.
    1. The selected option is highlighted.
    2. You can edit the option before applying it.
5. Click `apply to post`
    1. The button activates once you make a selection.
    2. The modal will close on it's own.
    3. You'll be directed to the title.
6. Check the title and **save the post**.

![](https://prod-files-secure.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/68e10e39-ef2a-4a05-9895-051e8245a633/b4e6a531-217f-43d5-adf0-9f55c1ca2a38/image.png)

**H1**

This is the first thing readers see when they open the article. Its job is to confirm they've found the right content.

- Maximum 70 characters
- Must contain the first keyword from the "Secondary Keywords" section
- Must be **different from the meta title** – it should offer a complementary perspective on the same topic, not just rephrase it
- Only one H1 per article

**How they work together:** The meta title attracts the reader from search results. The H1 confirms they've landed on the right page and matches their search intent.

*Example:*

- Meta title: "10 Tips to Learn Piano Faster"
- H1: "How to Improve Your Piano Skills at Home"

Same topic. Different words. Both useful to the reader.

**Generating an H1 with AI (upcoming feature)**

1. Make sure your post already has a title. The AI uses your title as context — without it, generation will return an error.
2. **Fill in the Secondary Keyword field** in the SEO AI Generator panel.
3. **Click the ✨ button** next to the field.
4. **Review the 5 H1 options** shown inside the modal (each option already includes your secondary keyword).
5. Select your preferred option by clicking it.
    1. The selected option is highlighted.
    2. You can edit the option before applying it.
6. Click `apply to post`
    1. The button activates once you make a selection.
    2. The modal will close on it's own.
    3. You'll be directed to the H1.
7. Check the H1 and save the post.

**Post Subtitle**

- Maximum **110 characters**
- Appears below the title on the article page
- Summarise the article's value proposition

**URL Structure**

> ⚠️ **Never change the URL of a published article.** This damages search traffic. If you believe a change is necessary, contact your manager first.

When creating a URL for a NEW article:

- Use **hyphens** between words (not underscores)
- Keep it short and descriptive
- Use a different keyword than the ones in your meta title or H1
- Should not contain any special characters
- Should not contain any stop words (a, the, is etc. [Find list here](https://countwordsfree.com/stopwords/)).

**GOOD URL:** ✅ `learn-guitar-london`
**BAD URL:** ❌ `guitar_lessons_in_london` → Uses underscores & stop words

> 🧱 **Chunking**
>
> For the body text, the writer should use the chunking technique for structuring content into smaller, more focused sections (called "chunks") that AI systems can more easily process and extract information from.
>
> In practical terms, this means that each paragraph should be able to stand alone and make sense without requiring context from surrounding paragraphs. Chunked content uses:
>
> - Clear and descriptive headings (H1, H2 & H3) to separate different subtopics
> - Paragraphs consisting of a maximum of three or four sentences (this is a guideline but not a strict rule), and that focus on one main idea at a time.
> - Alternates two or three text paragraphs with other Gutenberg blocks (AI systems can more easily extract and cite specific points from an info block or a bulleted list, for example).

## Translation Reference

At Superprof, our global network extends to more than **40 countries**, each with its own blog. However, only some of these countries produce original content on a consistent basis:

- France 🇫🇷
- Spain 🇪🇸
- UK 🇬🇧

Blogs from other countries generally focus on **translating and adapting articles from these sources.**

The process of adaptation is not merely changing a few words, but transforming the content so that it **resonates with the expectations and needs** of the **local audience**.

> ✅ Based on an analysis using tools such as Ahrefs or Semrush, the **Blog Manager** should **provide the essential keywords to the writers**. These keywords should be strategically integrated by the writer into the article, including the title, headings (H1, H2, etc.) and meta description.

However, tailoring content goes **beyond including keywords and adjusting the local dialect**.

The main task for which the service is being contracted is to **restructure and customise the article** to capture the specific interest of your local audience.

To better understand what your audience is searching for, we recommend **analysing Google's SERP** for specific search terms.

### Best Practices

- **Prioritise natural flow:** The text should read smoothly, even if that means stepping away from word-for-word translation
- **Adapt, don't just translate:** Localise idioms, quotes, cultural references, and examples
- **Stay consistent:** Use the same terms for recurring concepts throughout the article
- **Respect structure:** Keep the original headings, bullet points, and formatting unless there's a clear reason to change

### Media Guidelines

**Images**

- Replace culturally specific images (landmarks, currencies, school systems)
- Translate captions and alt text
- Adapt Canva visuals from the original — access the source file via STSEO Blog Groups section

**Videos & TikToks**

- Find equivalent videos in your language
- If no good alternative exists, leave it out or find different relevant media

**Links & References**

- Replace foreign-language links with high quality local equivalents.
- If no suitable local source exists, you can keep the original one if:
    - The content ads value
    - The content will be understood by the people in your market
    (*Example:* An english source for a country where most people understand English.)

# Linking

There are two types of links:

1. **Internal links** — Links to other pages on the Superprof website (blog articles and landing pages). These connect related content and help readers and search engines navigate our site.
2. **External links** — Links to other websites. When we link to trustworthy sources, search engines see this as a sign of quality content.

> ⚠️ **Links must be spread throughout the article** → never closer than 150 words apart.

## Internal linking

> 🛡️ **Branch** = group of articles
> **Branch article (identified with this symbol 🌿)** = Main article of a group of articles.

We publish articles in groups called **branches**.

Each branch has one **main article (or branch article 🌿)** and several supporting articles on the same topic.

**You must add links between all articles in your group of articles while writing.** The whole branch is published at the same time, so all links must be in place before publication.

**Linking rules:**

- **Main article (or branch article 🌿)** → links to all supporting articles
- **Each supporting article** → links back to all other supporting articles and the main article
- ❌ Articles never contain links to themselves.

```mermaid
🌿 Main Article (Branch article)
├── Secondary 1
├── Secondary 2
├── Secondary 3
└── Secondary 4
```

**Why this matters:** If linking is not done correctly, supporting articles become invisible to search engines and readers because they are not displayed on the Blog's Homepage. Only main articles (or branch articles 🌿) are displayed on the Blog's Homepage and readers (and crawlers) access the secondary articles through links once on the blog.

We also have 2 types of internal links:

- **Blog article links** — Links between blog posts. Add these while writing your article.
- **Landing page links** — Links to Superprof's payment pages. These should **never be added by you as a writer**.

> 💡 See Guide reference (landing page links handling) for instructions on what to do when you work with a blog post that contains links to landing pages.

### Anchor Text Rules

The **anchor text** is the clickable text that takes the user to a webpage.

**Rules:**

- Keep anchors between **3-5 words** (longer is acceptable, but never link full sentences)
- **No bold or italics**
- Anchors should tell the reader what the linked article is about
- **Use a different anchor text** each time you link to the same article

> **GOOD ANCHORS**
>
> - "Italian numbers and counting"
> - "how to tell the time"
> - "the days of the week"

> **BAD ANCHORS**
>
> - "click here" — tells the reader nothing
> - "this article" — too vague
> - "**Italian numbers**" — never bold anchors
> - "numbers" - too broad
> - Full sentence as a link — too long, looks spammy

### Example: Internal Linking in Practice

Here is how the internal linking could look for the following branch:

Basic Italian Vocabulary (1 main + 4 supporting articles)

```mermaid
🌿 Basic Italian Vocabulary (Branch article)
├── Describing The Weather In Italian
├── How To Tell The Time in Italian
├── Italian Days Of The Week
└── Numbers in Italian
```

**In the main article**, link to all supporting articles:

> "Italians love discussing the weather — it's a common conversation starter."
> …
> "You'll also want to learn how to tell the time for making plans, and knowing the days of the week helps with scheduling."
> …
> "Start with learning the Italian numbers to talk about prices and dates."

**In each supporting article**, link back to the main and to related articles:

> In "How To Tell The Time in Italian":
>
> - "See our basic Italian vocabulary guide" → main article
> - "You'll need Italian numbers to tell the time" → Numbers article
> - "Combine with days of the week to make appointments" → Days article

**Varying anchor text:** When four articles link to "Numbers in Italian," each uses different anchor text:

- Italian numbers
- how to count in Italian
- learn Italian numbers
- numbers in Italian

## External Linking

Link to external websites only when the link **adds clear value for the reader and comes from a verified and trustworthy source**.

> **DO ✅**
>
> - Link to the original source (study, statistic, official information).
> - Make sure link opens in new tab

> **DO NOT ❌**
>
> - Link to the homepage
> - Link to Wikipedia
> - Link towards our competitors

Most external links are used in the "Sources - References Block" at the end of your article.

# Image Requirements

**File Naming**

# Text Formatting

**Bold Text**

- Use sparingly to guide the reader's eye
- Bold only **key words or short phrases**—never complete sentences
- Don't overdo it—if everything is bold, nothing stands out

**Italics**

- Use only for **foreign language words**
- Don't use italics for emphasis (use bold instead)

**Paragraphs**

- Group ideas into **3-5 sentences per paragraph**
- No floating single sentences
- Keep sentences under **20 words**
- One idea per sentence

**Lists**

- **Bullet points:** Non-sequential items
- **Numbered lists:** Steps or ordered information
- **Tables:** Comparing information or summarising data

**Acronyms**

- Write the full term first, then introduce the acronym:
    - "Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)" → then "MIT"
    - "Saturated fatty acids (SFA)" → then "SFA"

**Numbers**

- Number formatting varies by market. Follow the convention used in your target country.
- Examples:
    - For large numbers: 9 000, 9.000, or 9,000
    - Decimals: 2,35 or 2.35
- If unsure, check a local news website or ask your Superprof contact.

**Brand Names**

Always use exact spelling and capitalisation as shown on the brand's official website: YouTube (not youtube), TikTok (not Tiktok).

**Capitalisation on titles**

This should only be done for the blogs in English.

# Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's a Problem |
|---|---|
| Links too close together | Looks spammy, hurts SEO |
| Anchor text = entire sentence | Unclear what the link is about |
| City name as anchor | Too vague, not descriptive |
| Linking to homepages | No specific value for reader |
| Extra quotes in Quote block | Block already adds quotation styling |
| Stock-photo style images | Look unnatural, reduce credibility |
| Image directly after H2 | Disrupts reading flow |
| Visual elements clustered together | Looks cluttered, poor UX |
| Forgetting CSS tags | Some blocks won't display correctly |
| Changing text size in Media+Text block | Breaks display—use Typography > Reset All to fix |

# Submitting Your Work

When you finish writing, you need to update the status in the blog order database. This tells your Superprof contact that your article is ready for review.

Status options:

| Status | Who Sets It | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Work in progress** | Writer | You are currently working on the article |
| **Pending review** | Writer | You have finished — the article is ready to be checked |
| **Correction needed** | Your Superprof contact | Changes are required — check the feedback and make corrections |
| **Published** | Your Superprof contact | The article is live on the blog |

1. Complete all items in the Pre-Publication Checklist below
2. Preview your article to check the final result
3. Go to your order in the blog order database
4. Change the status from **Work in progress** to **Pending review**

Your Superprof contact will review the article and either:

- Mark it as **Published** (no changes needed)
- Change it to **Correction needed** (with feedback on what to fix)

**If Corrections Are Needed**

1. Check the feedback from your Superprof contact (usually on Notion or via e-mail)
2. Make the requested changes in WordPress
3. Change the Notion status back to **Pending review**

> ⚠️ Always preview your article after making corrections to make sure everything displays correctly.

# Pre-Publication Checklist

SEO Elements

- [ ] Meta title under 60 characters with main keyword
- [ ] H1 under 70 characters with secondary KW
- [ ] Meta description under 155 characters with secondary KW
- [ ] URL is short, uses hyphens, includes keyword
- [ ] Meta title ≠ H1 ≠ meta description

Content

- [ ] YourText Guru score in green zone
    - [ ] DSEO under 2% → DSEO score under 2% (this measures duplicate content — shown in YourText Guru)
- [ ] OR; used SEMrush: related keywords, SEO and readability score.
- [ ] All acronyms defined on first use
- [ ] Tone fits local audience expectations
- [ ] Cultural references localised (schools, exams, names, customs)
- [ ] Idioms and expressions adapted (not literally translated)
- [ ] Terminology consistent throughout
- [ ] Videos relevant to local audience (or removed if not)

Links

- [ ] Internal links spaced 150-200 words apart
- [ ] Anchor text is 3-5 words, varied, not bolded
- [ ] External links open in new tab
- [ ] No links to Wikipedia or competitors

Images

- [ ] All images renamed descriptively with hyphens
- [ ] Alt text added to all images
- [ ] No images directly after H2 headings
- [ ] Visual elements spread throughout article

Formatting

- [ ] Paragraphs are 3-5 sentences
- [ ] Bold used sparingly on key phrases only
- [ ] Mandatory blocks included (info box, quote, images, etc.)
- [ ] Required CSS tags added for special blocks

Final Check

- [ ] Preview the article
- [ ] Visual elements not clustered
- [ ] Mobile display looks correct
